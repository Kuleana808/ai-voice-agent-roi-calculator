Auto repair after-hours drop-off and overnight intake script library

Use these as practical starting points, not legal, storage, or authorization policy advice. The job is simple: make it obvious where the customer or tow driver should leave the car, what information must be captured, and how the shop will follow up the next morning so an after-hours arrival does not turn into a lost or angry lead.

Key lockbox instructions

Tell the driver exactly how to finish an after-hours drop without confusion

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. If you are dropping the [Vehicle] off after hours, please park in [location], place the keys in our [lockbox / night-drop slot], and include your name, best callback number, and the main issue you want us to check.

If you want, text us once the vehicle is here so we can flag it for the morning team.
  • Best when customers reach voicemail and need a complete self-serve arrival path.
  • Reduces morning confusion around whose vehicle arrived and why.
Tow-in arrival

Make sure the tow handoff does not happen without contact info or problem notes

Hi, this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. If the [Vehicle] is being towed in after hours, please have the driver leave it in [location] and text or attach the customer name, callback number, key location, and any issue notes so we can pick it up first thing in the morning.
  • Useful for tow dispatch, roadside vendors, and emergency arrivals that happen when the front desk is closed.
  • Keeps the shop from inheriting an unidentified car with no callback trail.
Overnight confirmation

Reassure the customer their vehicle made it in and explain the next update window

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. We saw your [Vehicle] was dropped off after hours and it is checked into the morning queue.

We will review the concern and call or text you by [Time / Window] with the next update. If there is anything urgent you want the technician to know before then, reply here.
  • Best for calming anxious customers who dropped a vehicle overnight and are waiting for acknowledgment.
  • Turns a silent handoff into a professional next-step expectation.
Missing authorization

Recover the drop-off when the car arrived but the shop still needs approval details

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. Your [Vehicle] made it in, but before we begin we still need [authorization / signature / preferred contact number / key location confirmation].

Reply here or call us at [Phone Number] so we can keep the inspection moving this morning.
  • Useful when the customer dropped the car without enough paperwork or context.
  • Prevents the morning team from losing hours to incomplete intake.
Weekend arrival

Set expectations when the vehicle lands outside the shop’s normal review window

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. We received your after-hours drop-off for the [Vehicle]. Because it arrived during [weekend / holiday / after-hours queue], the first full review window is [next business day / time].

We will reach out as soon as the team has eyes on it. If your timing changed, reply here so we can note it for dispatch.
  • Best for preventing frustration when the customer assumes overnight means immediate diagnosis.
  • Protects trust without overpromising same-night action.
No-answer arrival recovery

Follow up after a missed call so the lead still knows how to drop the car cleanly

Hi [First Name], sorry we missed your call. If you still need to drop the [Vehicle] off after hours, park in [location], leave the keys in [lockbox / slot], and text your name plus the issue here.

We will confirm the vehicle in the morning and send the next update window.
  • Useful when the first inbound call was missed and the customer is deciding whether to tow somewhere else.
  • Converts a missed call into a usable arrival path fast.
Fleet / company vehicle intake

Capture the extra details business customers forget during after-hours drop-off

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. For the company vehicle drop-off, please include the driver name, company name, approval contact, best callback number, and anything the technician should know before diagnosis starts.

That helps us avoid delays once the morning team picks it up.
  • Best for fleet, contractor, or business-account work where one missing approver can stall the whole job.
  • Keeps the morning handoff from turning into approval-tag confusion.
Ready for morning triage

Close the loop once the shop can move from drop-off into diagnosis

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. We have your [Vehicle] in the morning triage queue and the team is ready to begin the first review step.

If you have any added symptoms, dashboard warnings, or timing constraints, text them here now so we can flag the repair correctly before the technician starts.
  • Useful for getting the last missing context before the job begins.
  • Bridges smoothly from after-hours intake into diagnostic workflow.
