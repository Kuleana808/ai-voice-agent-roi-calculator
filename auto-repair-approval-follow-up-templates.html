Auto repair approval follow-up script library

Use these as practical starting points, not compliance or legal advice. The job is simple: explain the recommended work clearly, ask for one concrete approval, reduce callback drift, and keep the repair order moving instead of losing momentum to voicemail tag or vague “just checking in” follow-up.

Estimate signoff

Follow up when the customer has the estimate but has not approved the work yet

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I’m following up on the estimate we sent for your [Vehicle]. We wanted to make sure you received it and see whether you want us to move forward with the recommended repair.

If you are ready to approve it, call or text us back at [Phone Number] and we can keep the job moving. If you still have questions about price, timing, or priority, we can walk through that with you.
  • Best for same-day or next-day follow-up after an estimate goes quiet.
  • Keeps the ask focused on one decision instead of a vague status check.
Diagnostic-to-repair

Move the customer from diagnostic findings into approved repair work

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] at [Shop Name]. We finished the diagnostic on your [Vehicle], and the next step is approval for the repair we discussed: [Repair Summary].

If you want us to proceed, reply here or call [Phone Number]. Once we have your okay, we can lock the job into the queue and avoid more delay.
  • Useful when the shop has already found the issue but the repair order is waiting on a yes/no answer.
  • Works well when advisors need a cleaner handoff from “we diagnosed it” to “we are cleared to fix it.”
Extra work approval

Ask for signoff on additional repair work uncovered after teardown or inspection

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. While working on your [Vehicle], we found an additional item that needs approval before we continue: [Extra Work Summary].

I wanted to reach you quickly so the job does not sit waiting in the bay. Call or text [Phone Number] if you want us to move forward or if you want to review the options first.
  • Best for add-on work, related repairs, or “while we are in there” recommendations that cannot wait until pickup.
  • Frames the decision around keeping the repair moving, not just upselling more work.
Priority / safety

Re-engage a customer who hesitates on a repair with safety or drivability impact

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] at [Shop Name]. I wanted to follow up on the repair recommendation for your [Vehicle], especially because [Safety / Drivability Note] is the part we do not want to leave unresolved.

If you want to approve the work now, call or text us at [Phone Number]. If timing or budget is the concern, we can talk through the best order to handle it.
  • Useful when the customer has not responded because they are delaying the decision, not because they missed the estimate.
  • Helps the advisor re-open the conversation without sounding pushy.
Warranty / third-party approval

Clarify when the next step depends on warranty or outside authorization

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. We are ready for the next step on your [Vehicle], but approval is still pending through [Warranty Company / Third Party / Fleet Contact].

We are following up from our side as well. If you have a faster contact or want to help move the approval along, call or text us at [Phone Number] and we will coordinate the next step.
  • Best for warranty admins, fleet approvals, and third-party repair authorizations that create front-desk bottlenecks.
  • Shows active follow-through without making the customer guess where the stall is happening.
Voicemail

Leave a short voicemail when you need repair approval back fast

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I’m calling about your [Vehicle]. We have the repair recommendation ready, and we need your approval before we can keep the job moving.

Please call or text us back at [Phone Number] when you get this. Again, this is [Name] from [Shop Name], and we’re ready for your approval on the next repair step.
  • Good when the customer does not answer but the repair cannot progress without authorization.
  • Keeps the callback reason specific so the voicemail is easier to act on.
Text follow-up

Send a concise approval reminder without a full phone call

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] at [Shop Name]. We are ready to move forward on your [Vehicle], but we still need approval for [Repair / Extra Work Summary]. Text or call [Phone Number] when you’re ready and we’ll keep it moving.
  • Useful for reducing callback lag when the customer only needs one simple reminder.
  • Works well after a voicemail or estimate email has already been sent.
End-of-day nudge

Close the day cleanly when approval did not come through before the shop closes

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] with [Shop Name]. I wanted to update you before we close for the day. We are still waiting on approval for [Repair / Extra Work Summary], so the vehicle will stay paused until we hear back.

If you want us to move forward first thing tomorrow, call or text [Phone Number]. We’ll pick it up right away once approval is in.
  • Best for keeping overnight stalls from becoming silent dead time.
  • Sets a clear next step instead of letting the customer assume the shop has already moved forward.

How to use these without sounding generic

  • Name the exact approval needed: estimate signoff, diagnostic-to-repair authorization, add-on work, safety-priority repair, or warranty clearance.
  • Give the customer one clear next step: approve now, ask questions, or call back to review price and timing.
  • When the repair is already paused, say that directly so the customer understands why callback speed matters.
