Auto repair declined-work follow-up script library

Use these as practical starting points, not compliance or customer-service policy advice. The job is simple: re-open the conversation, reduce silent declines, and give customers a clear next step when deferred maintenance, recommended repairs, and budget hesitation would otherwise die in the estimate follow-up pile.

Deferred maintenance follow-up

Re-open the conversation after recommended maintenance was postponed

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I wanted to follow up on the maintenance work we discussed for your [Vehicle].

If you still want to take care of it, call or text us back at [Phone Number]. If timing or budget is the only thing holding it up, we can help you map the best next step so the work does not keep getting pushed out.
  • Best for oil leaks, fluid services, tune-ups, and maintenance packages that were recommended but never scheduled.
  • Creates a gentle restart instead of sounding like a hard close attempt.
Recommended work hesitation

Follow up when the customer heard the estimate but never approved it

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] at [Shop Name]. I wanted to check in on the recommended work we reviewed for your [Vehicle].

If you still want to move forward, call or text us back at [Phone Number]. If you have questions about price, priority, or what can wait, we can walk through that with you so the estimate does not go stale.
  • Best when the customer listened to the findings but stopped short of approving the job.
  • Surfaces the real objection before the work disappears completely.
Budget timing

Follow up when the customer wants the work but is delaying on price or payday timing

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I’m following up on the estimate we prepared for your [Vehicle / Service Request].

If the only thing slowing this down is timing or budget, call or text us back at [Phone Number] and we can help you decide what should happen now versus what can be staged for later.
  • Useful when the customer says yes in principle but never schedules.
  • Keeps the shop in the conversation without pretending every line item is equally urgent.
Need-more-time follow-up

Re-engage customers who said “call me later” and then disappeared

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] with [Shop Name]. I wanted to circle back on the repair recommendations we discussed for your [Vehicle].

If you still want to get this handled, call or text us back at [Phone Number]. If you are waiting for a better time, we can help you plan the next best window before the issue becomes a bigger repair.
  • Useful when the customer never gave a hard no, only a soft delay.
  • Helps convert vague “later” into a real next action.
Safety urgency

Follow up when the declined work has real drivability or safety risk

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I wanted to follow up on the repair recommendations we flagged for your [Vehicle].

If you have not handled this yet, please call or text us back at [Phone Number]. We want to help you understand what should be fixed first so a safety or drivability issue does not turn into a bigger problem.
  • Best for brakes, tires, steering, battery, cooling, or check-engine issues that should not sit indefinitely.
  • Lets the shop sound responsible without sounding alarmist.
Seasonal reminder

Bring back deferred work when weather, mileage, or calendar timing changes

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I wanted to follow up on the maintenance or repair work we discussed for your [Vehicle], especially with [summer travel / winter weather / current mileage] coming up.

If you want to get ahead of it now, call or text us back at [Phone Number] and we can help you schedule the right next step.
  • Useful for tires, batteries, AC, cooling, belts, and seasonal maintenance that becomes urgent later.
  • Gives the follow-up a concrete reason instead of a random reminder.
Advisor catch-up

One more follow-up before the recommended work goes fully cold

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] with [Shop Name]. I wanted to make one more follow-up attempt on the repair recommendations for your [Vehicle] so we can get everything moving again.

Please call or text us back at [Phone Number] and we’ll help you with the fastest next step, whether that’s prioritizing the work, finding a better time, or scheduling the most urgent piece first.
  • Useful when the file is still open but every callback attempt keeps stalling.
  • Creates a simple reset message without forcing the customer to restart the whole conversation.
Voicemail + text combo

Short voicemail to pair with a declined-work SMS follow-up

Hi, this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I’m calling to follow up on the repair recommendations for your vehicle.

Please check the text message we just sent or call us back at [Phone Number] so we can help you decide the best next step. Thank you.
  • Good when the shop wants one standard voicemail that matches its declined-work text process.
  • Keeps the message brief while moving the customer into an easier reply channel.
