Auto repair estimate reminder and approval-recovery library

Use these as practical starting points, not legal, underwriting, or warranty advice. The job is simple: remind the customer what is waiting, make the next approval step obvious, and remove the advisor back-and-forth that happens when the estimate is ready but the work still is not sold.

Unsold estimate follow-up

Reopen the estimate without sounding pushy

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I wanted to follow up on the estimate for your [Vehicle / Repair].

If you want to move forward, reply APPROVE and I’ll confirm the next step, timing, and any parts needed. If you have questions before deciding, call or text [Phone Number] and I’ll walk you through it.
  • Best for estimates that were delivered but not yet approved.
  • Keeps the next step binary: approve now or ask a specific question.
Parts approval deadline

Prompt action before parts timing or slot availability slips

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] at [Shop Name]. We’re holding the estimate for your [Repair], but we still need your approval before we can order parts and lock the next opening.

If you want us to move forward, reply YES or call/text [Phone Number] today so we can keep timing on track.
  • Useful when a quote is stalling and the advisor needs a natural deadline.
  • Frames urgency around scheduling and parts flow instead of pressure.
Budget hesitation

Keep the estimate alive when the customer is unsure

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I know you were still deciding on the estimate for your [Vehicle].

If the only blocker is timing or budget, reply QUESTIONS and I’ll go over the options with you so you can decide what makes the most sense.
  • Best when the estimate stalled because the customer went quiet after hearing the number.
  • Opens the door to clarification without sounding defensive.
Inspection-to-repair callback

Convert the diagnostic result into a clean repair decision

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] at [Shop Name]. We finished the inspection on your [Vehicle], and your estimate is ready.

Reply CALL if you want me to go over the repair plan with you, or reply APPROVE if you want us to move ahead and schedule the work.
  • Useful when diagnostics are complete but the repair has not been sold yet.
  • Creates one direct approval path and one assisted path.
Insurance / third-party delay

Keep momentum while outside approvals are still pending

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. We’re still ready to move on your [Repair], but we need the final approval step completed before we can start.

If you already handled it, reply DONE and I’ll confirm timing. If not, call or text [Phone Number] and I’ll tell you exactly what is still pending.
  • Best when insurer, warranty, or third-party approvals create a vague stall.
  • Reduces dead airtime and gives the customer one easy reply option.
Advisor voicemail + text handoff

Use a short voicemail when the customer does not answer

Voicemail: Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I’m calling about the estimate for your [Vehicle]. Give me a call or text at [Phone Number] when you can and I’ll get you the next step.

Follow-up text: Just left you a voicemail about the estimate for your [Vehicle]. Reply here if you want to move forward or want me to explain any part of the quote.
  • Useful when the customer is not answering but the estimate still has near-term close potential.
  • Pairs well with the broader auto repair voicemail helper page.
Pending approval after estimate review

Confirm the customer is ready after you already discussed the quote

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] at [Shop Name]. Checking back after our estimate review for your [Vehicle].

If you want us to move ahead, reply APPROVE and I’ll finalize the next step. If you want to split out the highest-priority repair first, reply PRIORITY and I’ll map that out.
  • Best after a real phone conversation where the customer said they would think it over.
  • Gives an alternative path for partial-scope approval instead of total ghosting.
Reactivation after a stale quote

Restart the conversation after the estimate cooled off

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. We still have the estimate for your [Vehicle] on file, so I wanted to check whether you still want help with it.

Reply READY if you want us to reopen the job and confirm timing, or reply NOT NOW and I’ll close the loop on our side.
  • Useful for older unsold estimates that still have legitimate recovery potential.
  • Lets the advisor clear the board without sounding spammy.
