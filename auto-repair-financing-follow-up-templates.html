Auto repair financing follow-up and approval-recovery library

Use these as practical starting points, not lending, legal, or warranty advice. The job is simple: keep the approved repair alive, make the money path obvious, and remove the advisor back-and-forth that happens when the estimate is ready but the payment step still is not solved.

Payment-plan objection

Reopen the job when the customer wants the repair but not the full bill today

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I know the repair for your [Vehicle] may feel like a lot all at once.

If you want, I can text you the payment-plan or phased-repair options so you can see the easiest next step. Reply OPTIONS and I’ll send them over.
  • Best when the customer already trusts the repair need but freezes at the payment step.
  • Keeps the conversation moving without forcing an all-or-nothing decision.
Deposit rescue

Get the first payment locked so the job can actually start

Hi [First Name], [Name] from [Shop Name]. We are ready to move ahead on your [Vehicle] as soon as the deposit is handled.

Reply PAY LINK if you want me to resend the payment link, or CALL if you want me to explain the breakdown first.
  • Useful when the customer said yes but never completed the initial payment.
  • Turns vague delay into a concrete next action.
Declined financing follow-up

Keep the repair alive after the first financing path falls through

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I saw the first financing option did not go through for your [Vehicle], but that does not always mean the repair is dead.

Reply BACKUP if you want me to walk you through the backup option or the highest-priority version of the job first.
  • Useful when the first lender declines and the advisor needs a second path.
  • Prevents immediate drop-off after one failed approval.
Lender-status callback

Keep trust up while the outside financing decision is still pending

Hi [First Name], it is [Name] from [Shop Name]. We are still waiting on the financing update tied to your [Vehicle], and I did not want you wondering whether the job went quiet.

Reply STATUS if you want the latest update, or CALL if you want me to explain the current timing.
  • Good when the shop is waiting on a third-party approval and the customer assumes nobody is working the file.
  • Keeps the advisor proactive without overpromising.
Split-scope approval

Offer the highest-priority repair first when budget is the real blocker

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. If doing the full repair for your [Vehicle] right now is too much, we can separate the must-do work from the can-wait items.

Reply PRIORITY and I’ll send the safest reduced-scope option first.
  • Useful when the objection is cash timing rather than disagreement with the diagnosis.
  • Creates a recovery path instead of losing the full ticket.
Advisor voicemail + text handoff

Use a short voicemail when financing follow-up is getting ignored

Voicemail: Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I’m calling about the payment or financing next step for your [Vehicle]. Give me a call or text at [Phone Number] and I’ll make it simple.

Follow-up text: Just left you a voicemail about the payment or financing next step for your [Vehicle]. Reply here if you want the easiest option resent.
  • Useful when the customer is not answering but still has near-term close potential.
  • Pairs well with the broader auto repair voicemail helper page.
Approval after estimate review

Confirm the customer wants the repair once the budget path is clear

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] at [Shop Name]. Checking back after we reviewed the estimate for your [Vehicle].

If you want us to move ahead once the payment piece is handled, reply READY and I’ll finalize the next step. If you want the easiest option resent, reply RESEND.
  • Best after a real conversation where the customer said the price path was the only blocker.
  • Keeps the advisor from restarting the whole sales conversation.
Reactivation after a stalled money path

Restart the conversation after the customer disappeared around payment

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. We still have the repair plan for your [Vehicle] on file, so I wanted to check whether you still want help getting the next payment or financing step sorted out.

Reply READY if you want us to reopen it, or NOT NOW and I’ll close the loop on our side.
  • Useful for jobs that were close to approval but died when the payment path got awkward.
  • Lets the advisor clear the board without sounding spammy.
