Auto repair pickup-ready notification script library

Use these as practical starting points, not compliance or legal advice. The job is simple: confirm the vehicle is ready, explain the pickup window clearly, reduce missed handoffs, and keep finished work from sitting overnight because the customer never got a concrete next step.

Ready now

Tell the customer the vehicle is complete and ready for same-day pickup

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. Your [Vehicle] is ready for pickup.

We’re here until [Closing Time] today. If you want to grab it before we close, call or text [Phone Number] and we’ll have everything ready at the front desk.
  • Best for same-day completion updates when the main goal is getting the car released before close.
  • Keeps the next step concrete: pickup timing plus one callback number.
Cashier handoff

Set expectations around invoice, payment, and what happens at pickup

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] at [Shop Name]. Your [Vehicle] is finished and ready for pickup.

When you come by, we’ll review the final invoice, collect payment, and hand off the keys at the front desk. If you want us to text the total or help you plan timing, call or text [Phone Number].
  • Useful when the shop wants fewer surprises at the counter and fewer "what do I need when I arrive?" callbacks.
  • Works well for service advisors who need a cleaner handoff into cashier/release flow.
Final timing

Give a near-finished vehicle update with a firm expected pickup window

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. We’re wrapping up the last step on your [Vehicle] and expect it to be pickup-ready around [Time Window].

If that timing works for you, great. If you need a different pickup plan, call or text [Phone Number] and we’ll coordinate it before close.
  • Best when the vehicle is almost done and the shop wants to line up the release before the customer starts calling in.
  • Reduces front-desk churn by turning vague status checks into a clear pickup plan.
After-hours pickup

Explain the plan when the vehicle is done but pickup may happen after close or first thing tomorrow

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] at [Shop Name]. Your [Vehicle] is finished, but it looks like pickup may need to happen after hours or tomorrow morning.

Call or text [Phone Number] and we’ll confirm the best release plan, payment handoff, and key pickup instructions so nothing is left unclear.
  • Useful when the work is complete but release timing no longer fits the standard front-desk window.
  • Helps the advisor stay specific instead of leaving a vague "your car is done" message late in the day.
Voicemail

Leave a short voicemail that makes the pickup-ready reason obvious

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I’m calling to let you know your [Vehicle] is ready for pickup.

Please call or text us back at [Phone Number] so we can confirm timing before [Closing Time]. Again, this is [Name] from [Shop Name], and your vehicle is ready to go.
  • Good when the customer misses the call but the vehicle should not sit without a clear next step.
  • Keeps the callback reason specific so the message is easier to act on.
Text follow-up

Send a concise pickup-ready text without forcing a long phone call

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] at [Shop Name]. Your [Vehicle] is ready for pickup. We’re here until [Closing Time]. Text or call [Phone Number] if you want us to confirm the total, hold the keys at the front desk, or set a pickup window.
  • Useful for reducing phone tag when the customer only needs a simple ready-now update.
  • Works well after a voicemail or status call has already gone out.
Payment ready

Prompt the customer to finish payment and keep the release moving

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. Your [Vehicle] is ready for pickup, and we can get everything wrapped up as soon as payment is handled.

If you want the final amount before you head over, or need to confirm the best pickup time, call or text [Phone Number] and we’ll make the handoff easy.
  • Best for preventing finished jobs from sitting because the customer has not mentally prepared for the payment step.
  • Frames payment as part of a smooth release, not an awkward separate chase.
End-of-day release

Close the day cleanly when the vehicle is done but the customer has not confirmed pickup

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] with [Shop Name]. I wanted to update you before we close for the day. Your [Vehicle] is complete and ready for pickup, but we have not confirmed your timing yet.

If you want to pick it up today, call or text [Phone Number] before [Closing Time]. Otherwise, we can line up the release for tomorrow morning.
  • Best for keeping finished repair orders from becoming silent overnight carryovers.
  • Sets a clear next step instead of letting the customer guess whether the vehicle is truly ready.

How to use these without sounding generic

  • Name the exact release status: ready now, nearly done, waiting at the front desk, after-hours pickup, or end-of-day carryover.
  • Give the customer one clear next step: confirm pickup timing, ask for the final amount, or call back before close.
  • When payment, cashier handoff, or key release matters, say that directly so the customer knows how pickup will actually work.
