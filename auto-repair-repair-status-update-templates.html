Auto repair repair-status update script library

Use these as practical starting points, not compliance or customer-service policy advice. The job is simple: explain the delay clearly, preserve trust, keep approvals moving, and give the customer one concrete next step when repair-status communication would otherwise stall the job.

Delayed pickup

Explain when the vehicle will not be ready at the originally promised time

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I wanted to update you on your [Vehicle]. It is taking a little longer than expected, so it will not be ready at the original pickup time we discussed.

Right now we expect the next update around [Time / Day]. If you need to adjust pickup plans, call or text us back at [Phone Number] and we will keep you posted as soon as the timing tightens up.
  • Best for jobs that slip because another repair ran long, the bay is backed up, or an inspection uncovered extra work.
  • Sets a realistic next-update promise instead of leaving the customer guessing.
Technician progress

Give a calm progress check-in while work is still in the bay

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] at [Shop Name]. Quick update on your [Vehicle]: our technician is still working through the [inspection / diagnostic / repair], and I wanted to keep you informed before the day gets away from us.

We are still moving it forward. If anything changes on timing or approval needs, we will call or text you right away at this number.
  • Useful when the customer expects same-day movement but the job is still actively in process.
  • Prevents silent-status frustration without overpromising completion.
Parts delay

Reassure the customer when the job is waiting on parts

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I wanted to let you know your [Vehicle] is waiting on [Part / Parts], and that is the main reason the repair is not wrapped yet.

As soon as the part lands or we have a tighter ETA, we will update you. If you want to talk through timing or alternate pickup plans, call or text us back at [Phone Number].
  • Best for backordered parts, transfer delays, or supplier timing that the customer cannot see from their side.
  • Keeps the explanation concrete instead of sounding evasive.
Approval waiting

Explain that the next step depends on customer or warranty approval

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] at [Shop Name]. We have your [Vehicle] checked in and the next step depends on approval for [repair / additional work / warranty-covered item].

If you are ready to move forward, call or text us back at [Phone Number]. Once we have approval, we can keep the repair moving instead of losing more time.
  • Useful for additional repair recommendations, warranty-covered items, and “please confirm before we proceed” moments.
  • Turns a vague status check into a clear action request.
End-of-day update

Close the day cleanly when the car stays overnight

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] with [Shop Name]. I wanted to update you before we close for the day. Your [Vehicle] is staying overnight so our team can finish [diagnosis / repair / testing] tomorrow.

We will contact you by [Time / Day] with the next status update. If you need anything tonight, reply here or call us at [Phone Number].
  • Best for overnight jobs, diagnostic holds, and repairs that need road testing or next-morning parts arrival.
  • Reduces end-of-day anxiety and unnecessary repeat calls.
Ready soon

Let the customer know pickup is close but not quite complete

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. Good news: your [Vehicle] is in the final stretch. We are finishing [testing / quality check / paperwork / cleanup] before release.

We expect to have the final pickup update for you around [Time]. If that timing changes, we will let you know right away.
  • Useful when the mechanical work is done but final checks or paperwork still stand between the customer and pickup.
  • Protects trust by not announcing “ready” too early.
Insurance / supplement

Update the customer when insurer or supplement timing is the blocker

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] at [Shop Name]. I wanted to update you that we are waiting on [insurance approval / supplement signoff / claim response] before the next repair step can move.

We are following up on our side as well. As soon as we have movement, we will contact you with the next timing update.
  • Especially useful for shops that handle light collision, warranty admin, or repair-authority delays.
  • Clarifies that the slowdown is administrative, not neglect.
Text follow-up

Short text when you need a fast status-touch without a full phone call

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. Quick update on your [Vehicle]: we are still waiting on [part / approval / technician completion], so it is not ready yet. We will update you again by [Time]. Text or call [Phone Number] if you need us sooner.
  • Good for reducing voicemail tag when the customer only needs a concise check-in.
  • Works as the text companion to a longer phone or voicemail workflow.

How to use these without sounding generic

  • Name the blocker clearly: parts delay, approval waiting, technician still diagnosing, quality check, or overnight hold.
  • Always give the next promised update window so the customer knows when they will hear from you again.
  • If the customer must act, ask for the exact approval or callback instead of hiding the action request inside a vague status message.
