Collision repair estimate follow-up script library

Use these as practical starting points, not legal or claims advice. The job is simple: get the customer or carrier moving, reduce silence after the estimate, and keep work from dying in the gap between inspection, approval, supplement, parts arrival, and scheduling.

Unsold estimate follow-up

Checking in after an estimate with no reply

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I wanted to follow up on the collision repair estimate we prepared for your [Vehicle].

If you still want to move forward, reply here or call us at [Phone Number] and we will help with the next step. If you are waiting on insurance, parts, or timing, let us know so we can keep the file moving.
  • Best 24 to 48 hours after the estimate when the customer went quiet.
  • Creates an easy reply path instead of forcing the customer to restart the conversation.
Insurance delay

Carrier callback when approval is holding the job

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] with [Shop Name]. We are following up on your repair file because we are still waiting on the insurance approval / next update needed to schedule the work.

If you have spoken with the adjuster already, reply here with any update. If not, we can tell you exactly what is still pending so you know the fastest next step.
  • Useful when the customer assumes the shop will call again after insurance moves.
  • Turns a vague stall into a specific action request.
Deductible hesitation

Keep the estimate alive when price shock slows approval

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I wanted to check whether anything is blocking the repair estimate we sent.

If the main question is deductible, timing, or what insurance will cover, reply here and we will walk through it with you before the estimate goes stale.
  • Best when the customer opened the estimate but has not committed.
  • Helps surface objections before the lead disappears completely.
Supplement approval

Update the customer when additional damage or supplement work appears

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. During repair planning we identified additional work that needs supplement approval before we can finish the job timeline.

We have already submitted the update. If you want, reply here and we will explain what changed and what we are waiting on so there are no surprises.
  • Works when silence after a supplement creates anxiety or second-guessing.
  • Reassures the customer that the file is active, not lost.
Parts delay

Parts-approval or arrival follow-up

Hi [First Name], quick update from [Shop Name]. Your repair file is active, and the current hold is [parts approval / parts arrival / backordered part timing].

We wanted to keep you updated so you are not left guessing. If you want the latest ETA or want to review alternate next steps, reply here and we will help.
  • Best for reducing inbound status calls while keeping trust high.
  • Prevents customers from assuming the shop stopped working the file.
Estimate expiration / urgency

Final nudge before the estimate goes cold

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] with [Shop Name]. I wanted to make one more follow-up attempt on your collision estimate before we close the file on our side.

If you still want to repair the vehicle, reply here and we will help you pick the fastest next step. If your plans changed, a quick reply also helps us keep our schedule accurate.
  • Use after earlier touches went unanswered.
  • Creates a clear close-or-continue moment without sounding aggressive.
Pickup / closeout

Repair-complete pickup coordination

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. Your vehicle is ready for pickup / almost ready pending the final approval item.

Reply here if you want to confirm pickup timing, payment questions, or any final insurance paperwork so we can close everything out smoothly.
  • Useful when final pickup coordination causes another avoidable delay.
  • Keeps the last-mile workflow from turning into another round of phone tag.
Advisor voicemail

Short voicemail for estimate recovery

Hi, this is [Name] from [Shop Name]. I am calling to follow up on your collision repair estimate / insurance update.

Please call or text us back at [Phone Number] and we will help with the next step, whether that is approval, scheduling, parts timing, or a quick estimate question. Thank you.
  • Good when the shop wants one standard voicemail that matches the text follow-up.
  • Pairs well with an SMS sent right after the voicemail drop.
