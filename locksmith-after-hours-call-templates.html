Locksmith after-hours callback template library

Use these templates to keep urgent lockout and dispatch jobs from going dark overnight. The goal is simple: acknowledge the missed call fast, collect just enough detail to route the next step, and preserve urgency without overpromising arrival times your team cannot hit.

Emergency lockout SMS

Immediate missed-call text for home or apartment lockouts

Hi [First Name] — this is [Business Name]. Sorry we missed your call.

If you are currently locked out, text back your address, whether it is a house or apartment, and the best callback number here. We'll follow up as fast as possible with the next available step.
  • Best when the caller may still be choosing between multiple locksmiths.
  • Collects the minimum dispatch detail without promising instant arrival.
Car unlock SMS

Vehicle lockout callback text with quick location capture

Thanks for calling [Business Name]. We missed your call, but we did get it.

If this is a car lockout, reply with your exact location, vehicle make/model, and the best callback number. We'll reach back out with the fastest next step we can.
  • Useful for roadside unlock demand where the exact location matters more than a long back-and-forth.
  • Helps the shop prioritize urgency and technician fit before the next callback.
Voicemail fallback

Short locksmith missed-call voicemail script

Hi, this is [Name] with [Business Name]. Sorry we missed your call.

Please text or call us back with your name, your location, and whether you need help with a lockout, rekey, car unlock, or another locksmith issue, and we'll get back to you as soon as we can. Thank you.
  • Good when after-hours voicemail catches the call before a tech or dispatcher can respond live.
  • Pairs well with an automatic SMS or manual text-back a minute later.
Overflow dispatch

Follow-up when the phones were slammed or every tech was busy

Hi [First Name] — this is [Business Name]. We missed your call while our team was tied up on active service calls.

If you still need a locksmith, reply with your location plus a quick note on the job and we will get you the fastest next step we can.
  • Best during peak lockout windows, bad weather spikes, or roadside-assistance overflow.
  • Salvages demand without pretending the call was answered live.
Rekey inquiry

Next-step text for non-emergency rekey or hardware jobs

Thanks for reaching out to [Business Name]. We missed your call.

If you need rekey service, lock replacement, or hardware help, text back the property type, your city, and the best callback number. We'll follow up to help quote or schedule the next step.
  • Useful for separating non-emergency jobs from lockout dispatch demand.
  • Keeps slower sales jobs from disappearing just because the line rolled to voicemail.
Commercial service

Commercial callback for storefront, office, or access-control issues

Hi [First Name], this is [Business Name]. Sorry we missed your call.

If this is for a business, reply with the company name, address, and a short note on the issue — lockout, rekey, hardware problem, or access concern — and we'll get the right person back to you.
  • Helps route higher-value commercial work without losing the job to a competitor.
  • Captures enough information to hand off cleanly between dispatcher, owner, and field tech.
Next-morning follow-up

Morning text to rescue overnight missed calls

Good morning — this is [Business Name]. We missed your call overnight and wanted to follow up.

If you still need locksmith help, reply here with your location and what you need, and we'll help you with the fastest next step available today.
  • Best for shops that do not fully cover overnight but want to recover morning demand quickly.
  • Simple enough for an owner, CSR, or dispatcher to send in batches.
Roadside ETA reset

Expectation-setting text when there will be a callback delay

Hi [First Name] — this is [Business Name]. We received your locksmith request.

Our team is assisting active jobs right now, but if you still need help, reply with your exact location and issue type and we'll update you with the next available step as soon as possible.
  • Useful when demand is real but you need to protect trust while buying time.
  • Sets expectations better than silence, especially for roadside unlock calls.
