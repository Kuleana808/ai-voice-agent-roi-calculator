Orthodontic reminder and follow-up template library

Use these as practical starting points, not polished marketing copy. The job is simple: reduce no-shows, keep parents informed, make prep clear, and stop consult or start-case demand from falling through the cracks when the front desk gets overloaded.

New-patient consult

Confirm the first orthodontic consult without friction

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. Your orthodontic consult is scheduled for [Day, Date] at [Time].

Please reply YES to confirm, or text/call us here if you need to reschedule. We look forward to seeing you.
  • Use right after booking to create a clear confirmation trail.
  • Best for consult-heavy practices where no-shows kill treatment-start volume.
24-hour reminder

Remind the patient what to bring before the appointment

Reminder from [Practice Name]: your orthodontic appointment is tomorrow at [Time].

Please bring your insurance card, referral if needed, and any records or forms we requested. Reply here with any questions before your visit.
  • Reduces incomplete arrivals and last-minute confusion.
  • Works for consults, records appointments, and treatment-start visits.
Parent / guardian follow-up

Keep the decision-maker loop closed before the visit

Hi [Parent First Name] — just confirming [Patient First Name]'s appointment with [Practice Name] on [Day] at [Time].

If anything changed with school, transportation, or timing, text us here and we will help with next steps.
  • Useful when attendance depends on parent logistics, not just patient intent.
  • Helps reduce same-day cancellations tied to scheduling confusion.
Records / imaging prep

Set up records appointments so they do not become avoidable no-shows

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. Just a reminder that your records appointment is on [Day] at [Time].

Please arrive [X] minutes early so we can stay on schedule for photos, scans, and next-step planning.
  • Best for practices where records visits are operationally important but easy for families to underrate.
  • Clarifies why the appointment matters without sounding heavy.
Treatment-start follow-up

Reopen the case when the consult went well but the start date still stalled

Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. I wanted to follow up after your orthodontic consult.

If you are ready to schedule the next step for braces or aligner treatment, reply READY and we will help you lock in the best time.
  • Useful when the consult converted emotionally but the operational next step never got booked.
  • Bridges consult demand into started treatment instead of letting it cool off silently.
Retainer issue / urgent callback

Respond fast when the inbound issue is urgent but not a full emergency

Hi [First Name] — we got your message for [Practice Name]. If this is about a broken retainer, loose wire, or discomfort issue, text us a quick summary and the best callback number here.

We will help with the fastest next step.
  • Useful for after-hours calls and front-desk overflow where patients need acknowledgment quickly.
  • Keeps urgent call volume from disappearing into voicemail silence.
No-show recovery

Restart the conversation after a missed appointment without sounding punitive

Hi [First Name], this is [Practice Name]. We missed you today and wanted to make it easy to get rescheduled.

Reply RESCHEDULE and we will send the next available options, or text us here if another timing issue came up.
  • Good for consults, records visits, and active-treatment appointments.
  • Keeps the tone helpful while recovering the next step fast.
Voicemail + text handoff

Use a short fallback when reminders and callbacks are getting ignored

Voicemail: Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. I am calling about your orthodontic appointment or next-step scheduling. Please call or text us back at [Phone Number] and we will help.

Follow-up text: Just left you a voicemail from [Practice Name]. Reply here if you want help confirming, rescheduling, or getting the next orthodontic step booked.
  • Useful when the front desk needs one consistent no-answer fallback.
  • Pairs well with the broader missed-call template page for after-hours coverage.
