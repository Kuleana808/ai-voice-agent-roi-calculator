Orthodontic retainer-repair and emergency triage script library

Use these as practical starting points, not medical advice. The job is simple: calm the patient, capture the urgency correctly, route the right next step, and keep after-hours orthodontic issues from turning into dropped callbacks or overloaded morning schedules.

After-hours text back

We got your message and will help you triage the retainer issue

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. We received your message about the retainer issue.

Please reply with a short description of what happened and a photo if you can safely send one. If there is severe pain, swelling, bleeding, or trauma, call the appropriate emergency line now. Otherwise we will review this and tell you the best next step as soon as possible.
  • Best first touch for voicemail or text intake outside normal hours.
  • Creates a calm handoff into photo triage instead of ad hoc back-and-forth.
Broken wire / poking wire

Send us a photo so we can decide if you need same-day relief

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. I am sorry you are dealing with the broken or poking wire.

Please text us a clear photo if possible and tell us whether the wire is causing active pain or irritation. Once we review it, we can tell you whether this looks like a same-day visit, a next-day visit, or a temporary comfort step until we see you.
  • Useful when the real job is sorting discomfort level fast.
  • Helps the team separate urgent chair time from lower-severity callbacks.
Lost retainer

We can help you avoid a long delay without overpromising

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. We got your message that the retainer is lost.

Reply with when you last wore it and whether anything feels like it has already shifted. We will help you figure out whether you need the next available visit, a replacement workflow, or another immediate next step.
  • Useful when the patient mainly wants clarity on urgency.
  • Keeps the response operational instead of sounding generic.
Cracked / broken retainer

Tell us if the appliance is wearable or painful right now

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. Thanks for letting us know about the cracked or broken retainer.

Please reply with a photo and let us know whether it is still wearable, feels sharp, or is no longer fitting correctly. That will help us tell you the fastest and safest next step.
  • Helps the team route replacement vs discomfort cases differently.
  • Good for morning backlog clean-up after overnight messages.
Morning callback triage

We reviewed your photo and want to hold the right appointment type

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. We reviewed the retainer issue you sent over.

We would like to [hold the next available repair visit / call you with a quick update / have you come in today if a relief slot opens]. Please reply with the best number to reach you and whether you can come in on short notice if needed.
  • Useful for converting overnight leads into a real next-step decision first thing in the morning.
  • Keeps same-day capacity flexible without making promises the practice cannot keep.
Same-day relief slot

We may be able to squeeze you in if the discomfort is active

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. If the retainer or wire issue is causing active discomfort, we may be able to work you into a same-day relief slot.

Reply YES if you can come in on short notice today, and include the best phone number for a quick callback. If not, we will coordinate the next best time.
  • Useful when there is a cancellation-fill opportunity.
  • Turns urgency into a schedulable action instead of open-ended tag.
Voicemail fallback

Just left you a voicemail about the retainer issue

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. We just left you a voicemail about the retainer or wire issue.

If it is easier, reply here with a photo and a short note about what is happening, and we will route the next step from there.
  • Useful when phone tag starts slowing triage down.
  • Pairs well with practices that want text-first after-hours recovery.
Next-day reassurance

We have you in the queue and will not let this disappear

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. We have your retainer issue queued for review and will follow up with the best next step as soon as the clinical team reviews it.

If anything gets worse before then, reply here with an updated photo or note so we can reassess the urgency.
  • Useful when the team needs one consistent promise for non-severe issues.
  • Reduces anxious duplicate messages while preserving a clean callback queue.
