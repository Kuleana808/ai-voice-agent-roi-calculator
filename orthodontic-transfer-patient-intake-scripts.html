Orthodontic transfer patient intake script library

Use these as practical starting points, not legal or insurance advice. The job is simple: get the right records quickly, keep the family clear on next steps, protect treatment continuity, and stop transfer inquiries from stalling because no one owns the follow-up.

New transfer inquiry

Welcome the family and set expectations for the first review

Hi [Parent First Name] — this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. Thank you for reaching out about transferring [Patient First Name]'s orthodontic care.

The next best step is for us to review the current records and treatment details so we can tell you exactly what the handoff would look like. You can reply here or call us at [Phone Number], and we will help you move this forward.
  • Use right after a website form, voicemail, or inbound transfer inquiry.
  • Sets a calm tone for families who are already dealing with disruption or a move.
Records request

Ask the prior orthodontic office for what the new team needs

Hello — this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We are helping [Patient First Name, Last Initial] establish orthodontic care with our office.

Could you please send the most recent records available, including treatment notes, X-rays, photos, scans/models if available, and the current treatment plan summary? If there is a release form you need from the family, send it to us and we will help complete it quickly.
  • Useful for front desks who need a clean, professional records-chase script.
  • Helps speed up consult readiness without repeated back-and-forth.
Parent callback

Explain what happens before a treatment-takeover consult gets booked

Hi [Parent First Name] — this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We can absolutely help you evaluate a transfer for [Patient First Name].

Before we schedule the next step, we want to gather the current treatment information so the doctor can review what has already been done and what still needs to happen. If you have the last office's contact info and any recent records, text or email them to us and we will take it from there.
  • Good when parents want a same-day answer but the chart review is the real dependency.
  • Prevents avoidable friction by making the process feel guided instead of vague.
Insurance / benefits transfer

Clarify benefits questions without overpromising

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. We received your transfer inquiry and are happy to help.

If insurance or orthodontic benefits are part of the decision, send us the subscriber details and any coverage information you already have. Once we review the case and the benefits details, we can tell you what we know so far and what still needs confirmation before the next step.
  • Useful when the transfer stalls because the family is unsure what coverage carries over.
  • Keeps the tone accurate while still moving the conversation forward.
Retainer / continuity question

Handle the family who needs coverage before the full takeover visit

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. We are reviewing your transfer request.

If the immediate concern is a retainer issue, broken appliance, or treatment continuity question before the full consult, text us a photo and a short note about what changed. We will review it and let you know the fastest next step.
  • Best when the transfer inquiry includes an urgent comfort or compliance concern.
  • Pairs well with a separate retainer-emergency or broken-wire triage workflow.
Chart reviewed, book next step

Move the transfer patient into a start-of-care or takeover visit

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. We reviewed the transfer information we have so far and would like to schedule the next visit with our team.

Call or text us at [Phone Number] and we will help you choose the best appointment time for the doctor to review the case and map the next step in care.
  • Useful after records arrive and the practice is ready to convert the inquiry into a booked visit.
  • Keeps the CTA simple so the family knows the process is finally moving.
Mid-treatment emergency transfer

Route urgent transfer friction without promising a full answer on the spot

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. I am sorry you are dealing with that in the middle of treatment.

Please text us a photo of the issue and tell us whether the problem is pain, a broken wire, a loose bracket, or a retainer concern. We will review it and tell you whether the next step should be urgent relief guidance, a quick visit, or a full transfer evaluation.
  • Helps the team respond quickly when a transfer lead starts with an urgent problem instead of a clean intake path.
  • Creates a bridge between transfer intake and triage workflows.
Dormant transfer reactivation

Revive the inquiry when the family stopped replying mid-handoff

Hi [First Name] — this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. I wanted to follow up on your orthodontic transfer inquiry.

If you still need help moving care for [Patient First Name], reply here with the best phone number and the prior office's name. We can tell you the fastest way to get the records and schedule the right next step.
  • Good for stale transfer leads where the family meant to move forward but got distracted by logistics.
  • Pulls the conversation back to one actionable next step.
