Orthodontic treatment acceptance script library

Use these as practical starting points, not legal or financial advice. The job is simple: make the next step feel clear, surface the real blocker quickly, and keep diagnosed treatment from going dark because no one owns the callback sequence after the consult.

Unscheduled start-case follow-up

We still have your orthodontic plan ready when you are

Hi [First Name] — this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. I wanted to follow up because we still have your orthodontic treatment plan open and can help you choose the best next step.

If you would like, we can review timing, start-date options, and anything that still feels unclear. Call or text us back at [Phone Number] and we will make it easy.
  • Useful when the consult went well but the patient never picked a start date.
  • Keeps the tone helpful instead of pushy.
Financing objection

Let us walk through the monthly-payment path with you

Hi [First Name] — this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. I am following up on your orthodontic treatment plan.

If cost or monthly-payment questions are the main thing slowing down the decision, call or text us back at [Phone Number]. We can review the payment options and help you figure out the most workable path without starting over.
  • Best when the patient shows interest but keeps stalling after the fee discussion.
  • Frames the callback around clarity, not pressure.
Insurance + benefits follow-up

We can review coverage questions before you schedule the start

Hi [First Name] — this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We are following up on your orthodontic treatment plan.

If insurance coverage, benefits, or out-of-pocket questions are holding things up, call or text us at [Phone Number]. We can review what we know so far and tell you what details we still need to finalize the next step.
  • Useful when the real blocker is benefits confusion, not lack of interest.
  • Pairs well with a separate benefits-verification workflow.
Parent / guardian delay

Tell us what still needs to be decided at home

Hi [Parent First Name] — this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. I wanted to follow up on [Patient First Name]'s orthodontic treatment plan.

If timing, school schedule, transportation, payment questions, or next-step planning are still being discussed at home, text or call us at [Phone Number]. We can help you sort through the part that is still unresolved.
  • Useful when the patient is a minor and the delay is really a family logistics issue.
  • Helps uncover the true objection instead of letting the case sit quietly.
Consult complete, no start date

We can still hold the momentum from your consult

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. We wanted to check in because your orthodontic consult is complete, but we have not yet scheduled the treatment start.

If you would like to keep things moving, reply here or call us at [Phone Number] and we can review the next available start dates and what the first visit would look like.
  • Good when the practice wants a cleaner bridge between consult and case start.
  • Creates gentle urgency without sounding scripted.
Missed callback recovery

We tried reaching you and can still help with the next step

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. We tried reaching you about your orthodontic treatment plan and did not want your next step to get lost.

Call or text us back at [Phone Number] when it is convenient. We can answer questions, review the plan again, and help you decide whether now is the right time to schedule.
  • Useful after voicemail tag or when treatment-coordinator callbacks keep failing.
  • Keeps the door open without sounding like a breakup message.
Start-date urgency

Reach out before the most workable appointment slots disappear

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. We still have your orthodontic treatment plan ready, and I wanted to reach out before the next set of start-date openings fills.

If you want to move forward, call or text us at [Phone Number] and we can review the best timing options now.
  • Best when the practice genuinely has limited preferred starts for school, travel, or provider schedule reasons.
  • Adds urgency without using fake countdown tactics.
Reactivation after a pause

It is okay if the timing changed — we can restart the conversation cleanly

Hi [First Name] — this is [Practice Name]. I know the timing may not have worked when we first discussed your orthodontic treatment plan.

If you want to revisit it now, call or text us at [Phone Number]. We can review the plan again, answer fresh questions, and help you decide on the right next step without repeating the whole process from scratch.
  • Useful for older unscheduled cases that still have real intent.
  • Reopens the conversation without making the patient feel chased.

How to make these scripts convert better

  • Route callbacks by blocker, not only by age: financing, insurance, parent timing, and scheduling delays need different wording.
  • Have one clear owner for unscheduled-start follow-up so cases do not drift between coordinator, front desk, and doctor notes.
  • Pair every script with one easy next step: reply by text, pick a callback window, or review start-date options.
  • When the callback volume becomes too large to manage consistently, move the practice into the orthodontics ROI calculator and workflow-plan CTA.
