Pediatric dental broken appointment recovery script library

Use these as practical starting points, not legal or clinical advice. The job is simple: make it easy for parents to re-engage, remove friction around rescheduling, protect same-day openings, and keep broken appointments from turning into long-term patient leakage because no one owns the save sequence.

Failed confirmation follow-up

We did not receive confirmation and wanted to help you keep the visit

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We did not receive confirmation for [Child First Name]'s appointment and wanted to check whether you still want to keep that visit.

Call or text us back at [Phone Number]. If another time works better, let us know and we will help you switch it before the appointment slot is lost.
  • Best for reminder sequences that ended in silence.
  • Keeps the tone helpful instead of sounding like a penalty notice.
Same-day cancellation save

We can help you reschedule quickly so care does not stall

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. I saw that [Child First Name]'s appointment changed today and wanted to make it easy to grab the next best option.

Call or text us back at [Phone Number] and we can help you pick a new day or time right away so the visit does not keep getting pushed out.
  • Useful when a family cancels the day of and the team wants to preserve momentum.
  • Strong for school, illness, and transportation-related changes.
No-show callback

We wanted to check in after the missed visit and help with the next step

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We missed seeing [Child First Name] for the scheduled visit and wanted to check in.

If you still want help getting the appointment back on the calendar, call or text us at [Phone Number]. We can look at the next best options and make the reschedule process easy.
  • Useful right after a missed visit when the family may feel awkward calling back.
  • Reopens the conversation without blame-heavy language.
School or timing conflict

Tell us the better timing window and we will work around it

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We know school schedules, sports, and parent workdays can make appointments hard to keep.

If timing was the reason [Child First Name]'s visit broke, call or text us back at [Phone Number] with the days or times that usually work better and we will help you find the next fit.
  • Best when the real issue is logistics, not lack of interest.
  • Helps the team collect better reschedule data instead of just retrying blind.
No-response second touch

We wanted to try one more time before the appointment follow-up goes cold

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. I wanted to follow up one more time because we have not heard back about [Child First Name]'s missed or cancelled appointment.

If you still want help rescheduling, call or text us at [Phone Number]. Even if you are not ready yet, we can note the best timing window for the next follow-up.
  • Useful after the first save attempt gets no answer.
  • Gives the team a clean persistence script without sounding aggressive.
Same-week refill

We have another opening if you want to recover the visit sooner

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We had another opening come up and wanted to check whether you would like to use it for [Child First Name]'s missed or cancelled visit.

If you want that option, call or text us back at [Phone Number] as soon as you can and we will see whether it still matches your schedule.
  • Strong for turning cancellations into recovered chair time.
  • Adds a concrete reason for the parent to reply now.
Prep or sedation breakdown

If prep confusion caused the break, we can walk through it before rebooking

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We are checking in because [Child First Name]'s appointment may have been affected by prep questions or visit-day uncertainty.

If fasting, medications, arrival timing, or visit instructions were the blocker, call or text us at [Phone Number]. We can review the details before you rebook so the next appointment feels easier to keep.
  • Useful for sedation, treatment, or procedure visits where prep confusion causes broken appointments.
  • Moves the callback from generic scheduling into problem-solving.
Repeat broken appointments

Let us reset the follow-up plan instead of repeating the same cycle

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. I know it can be frustrating when appointments keep getting moved or missed.

If it would help, call or text us back at [Phone Number] and we can figure out the best timing, reminder style, and next-step plan for [Child First Name] so the next visit is easier to keep.
  • Best for families with repeat cancellations or no-shows.
  • Positions the practice as collaborative instead of corrective.
