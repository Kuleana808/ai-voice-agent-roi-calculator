Pediatric dental overdue recall reactivation script library

Use these as practical starting points, not legal or clinical advice. The job is simple: make it easy for families to restart care, remove friction around overdue visits, surface the real blocker quickly, and keep hygiene or diagnosed treatment from going dark because nobody owns the callback sequence.

Overdue hygiene recall

We would love to help [Child First Name] get back on the schedule

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We are reaching out because [Child First Name] is overdue for a visit and we would love to help you get the next appointment back on the calendar.

Call or text us back at [Phone Number], and if certain days or times work best, let us know so we can make the next step as easy as possible.
  • Best for broad hygiene recall outreach where the family quietly drifted.
  • Keeps the tone warm and low-pressure.
No-response follow-up

We wanted to follow up one more time before the list goes cold

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. I wanted to follow up because we have tried to reconnect about [Child First Name]'s overdue visit.

If you still want help scheduling, call or text us back at [Phone Number]. We can look at the next best options and help you pick a time that works.
  • Useful after texts or first callbacks go unanswered.
  • Gives the team a clean second-touch script without sounding aggressive.
Unscheduled treatment

We can help you restart the treatment plan without starting over

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We are following up because [Child First Name]'s treatment plan is still open on our side.

If you want to move forward, call or text us back at [Phone Number]. We can review what still applies, what might need to be updated, and the fastest path to getting the next appointment scheduled.
  • Useful for diagnosed work that fell into an unscheduled list.
  • Reduces the fear that the whole process has to restart from zero.
Insurance or cost hesitation

Tell us if insurance or timing is the part slowing things down

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We are checking in about [Child First Name]'s overdue visit or open treatment plan.

If insurance questions, timing, cost, or visit details are the main reason it has not been scheduled yet, call or text us back at [Phone Number]. We can walk through the part that feels unclear and help you decide on the best next step.
  • Useful when the real blocker is uncertainty, not lack of interest.
  • Helps staff surface objections faster.
Same-week save

We have an opening if you want to get the visit handled sooner

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We had an opening come up and wanted to check whether you would like to bring [Child First Name] in sooner for the overdue visit or pending treatment.

If you want that spot, call or text us back at [Phone Number] as soon as you can and we will see whether it still fits your schedule.
  • Strong for turning cancellations into recovered production.
  • Adds a real scheduling reason to reply now.
Lapsed family reactivation

We are happy to help your family come back whenever you are ready

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. It has been a while since we last saw [Child First Name], and we wanted to check in.

If you would like help getting back on track with care, call or text us at [Phone Number]. We can help with the next available appointment and answer any questions before you book.
  • Useful for families who feel awkward returning after a long gap.
  • Positions the office as helpful instead of corrective.
Recall list cleanup

Can we confirm whether you still want us to keep [Child First Name] on the active recall list?

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We are reviewing our recall list for [Child First Name] and wanted to make sure we have the right next step.

If you still want help scheduling the next visit, call or text us back at [Phone Number]. If another timing window would work better, let us know and we can note that for the next follow-up.
  • Useful for cleaning stale lists without sounding transactional.
  • Helps the team sort active interest from dead records.
Treatment coordinator callback

Let us review the overdue visit or treatment plan in one quick call

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. I know overdue visits and pending treatment plans can pile up quickly.

If it would help, call or text us back at [Phone Number] and we can review the visit type, timing, prep, and scheduling options in one quick conversation so you know exactly what to expect next.
  • Works well when parents need reassurance more than another reminder.
  • Turns a stale list touch into a decision-support conversation.
