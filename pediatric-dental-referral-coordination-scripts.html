Pediatric dental referral coordination script library

Use these as practical starting points, not legal or clinical advice. The job is simple: reduce phone tag, sound organized to parents and partner offices, collect the details your team actually needs, and keep high-intent treatment or specialty referrals from stalling because no one owns the next callback.

Parent callback

We received your referral and need one scheduling detail

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We received [Child First Name]'s referral and want to help with the next step.

Please call or text us back at [Phone Number] with the best day or time for the appointment and any deadlines your referring office mentioned. If you have the referral paperwork or insurance card handy, that will help us move faster when we reconnect.
  • Useful when the parent expects the referral alone was enough to lock in the visit.
  • Keeps the callback tied to scheduling momentum instead of vague status updates.
Referring office follow-up

Need records or x-rays before we offer the next appointment

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We are following up on [Child First Name]'s referral.

Before we finalize the next appointment step, we still need [x-rays / referral notes / treatment summary / insurance information]. Please send those to [Fax / Secure Email] or call us back at [Phone Number] so we can keep the handoff moving.
  • Helps staff sound clear when a missing document is blocking the schedule.
  • Works for general-dentist, pediatrician, oral-surgery, and specialty referral loops.
Insurance + referral

Referral received but benefits details are still missing

Hi, this is [Name] with [Practice Name]. We have [Child First Name]'s referral, but we still need a few insurance details before we can confirm the next visit.

Please call or text us back at [Phone Number] with the insurance provider, subscriber name, and any referral instructions you were given. That lets us line up the referral and benefits check in one step.
  • Best when the parent assumes the specialist handoff already covered insurance details.
  • Prevents referral coordination and benefits verification from becoming two separate callback loops.
Urgent specialist handoff

Same-day or priority referral callback

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. I am calling back about [Child First Name]'s referral and want to make sure we help with the next step as quickly as possible.

Please call us back at [Phone Number] and let us know whether this is a same-day concern, pain issue, swelling concern, or a timing-sensitive specialist visit. If the referring office gave you paperwork or instructions, keep that nearby when we reconnect.
  • Useful for urgent tooth-pain, trauma, sedation, or oral-surgery-adjacent handoffs.
  • Creates a cleaner triage path than a generic “we got your referral” voicemail.
No-answer fallback

Second-attempt referral status voicemail

Hi, this is [Name] with [Practice Name]. We are following up again about [Child First Name]'s referral.

When you have a moment, please call or text us back at [Phone Number] with the best callback window and any referral details you want us to review before the appointment. We want to keep the handoff moving without making you restart the whole process.
  • Useful after the first handoff call fails and the family still has not re-engaged.
  • Protects momentum for higher-value consults or treatment visits.
Office-to-office handoff

Confirming the specialist handoff with the referring team

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We are coordinating [Child First Name]'s referral and want to confirm we have the right next-step details.

Can you confirm the referral reason, any records or x-rays already sent, and whether the family was told to expect a call from our team? If anything is still outstanding, please send it to [Fax / Secure Email] or call us at [Phone Number].
  • Helps avoid misaligned expectations between the family and the referring office.
  • Useful when multiple teams touch the handoff and nobody owns the final callback.
Recall + specialist return

Follow-up after a referral or treatment recommendation

Hi, this is [Name] from [Practice Name]. We are checking in about [Child First Name]'s recommended next step after the referral or previous visit.

If you still need help with scheduling, paperwork, or insurance questions, please call or text us back at [Phone Number]. We can help you figure out the next appointment step without starting over.
  • Good for families who went quiet after a consult recommendation.
  • Brings the conversation back to a specific next step instead of a generic recall message.
