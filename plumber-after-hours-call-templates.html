Plumber after-hours callback template library

Use these templates to keep leaking jobs from going dark overnight. The goal is simple: acknowledge the missed call fast, collect enough triage detail to route the job, and preserve enough urgency that the lead still books when your team follows up.

Emergency SMS

Immediate missed-call text for leak or no-water issues

Hi [First Name] — this is [Business Name]. Sorry we missed your call.

If this is an active leak, no-water issue, or another urgent plumbing problem, text back your address, the issue, and the best callback number here. We'll follow up as fast as possible.
  • Best when someone may still be deciding which plumber to call next.
  • Collects just enough detail to prioritize emergencies without promising impossible response times.
After-hours SMS

Night-and-weekend callback expectation setter

Thanks for calling [Business Name]. We missed your call, but we did get it.

If this is urgent, text back your address plus a quick note on the issue. If it can wait until morning, we will reach back out after [Opening Time] to help schedule the next step.
  • Useful when you want to separate emergency jobs from next-day scheduling.
  • Reduces lead drop-off by giving callers a clear path instead of silence.
Voicemail fallback

Short plumber missed-call voicemail script

Hi, this is [Name] with [Business Name]. Sorry we missed your plumbing call.

Please text or call us back with your name, address, and a quick note on the problem, and we'll get back to you as soon as we can. Thank you.
  • Good when the missed call triggered after-hours voicemail instead of a live answer.
  • Pairs well with an SMS sent right after the voicemail drop.
Overflow dispatch

Dispatch-board follow-up when the phones were slammed

Hi [First Name] — this is [Business Name]. We missed your call while our team was tied up on live jobs.

If you still need plumbing help, reply with your address and a quick summary of the problem. We'll get you the fastest next step we can.
  • Best during busy mornings, freeze days, or storm spikes when CSR pickup drops.
  • Helps salvage overflow leads without pretending the call was answered.
Leak triage

Photo-and-location request for live water issues

Thanks for reaching out to [Business Name]. If you're dealing with an active leak, please text your address plus a photo or short note on where the water is coming from if you can do so safely.

That helps us route the fastest callback.
  • Useful when your team needs triage detail before promising a dispatch order.
  • Turns vague panic calls into more usable routing context.
Next-morning save

Morning-after follow-up to revive missed overnight leads

Good morning — this is [Business Name] following up on your call from last night.

If you still need plumbing help, reply here or call us back and we can get you scheduled. If the issue was urgent and you already handled it, no problem — just let us know.
  • Best for preserving jobs that did not convert overnight but may still be open the next morning.
  • Creates a practical bridge from after-hours silence into bookable dispatch.
Estimate / non-emergency

Non-urgent scheduling callback for standard plumbing jobs

Hi [First Name] — sorry we missed your call to [Business Name].

If this is for a non-emergency plumbing issue or estimate, reply with your zip code and a short note on the job. We'll follow up with the best scheduling option.
  • Useful for drain cleaning, fixture installs, water heater quotes, or other standard jobs.
  • Keeps routine leads from being treated like emergency dispatches.
Owner cell backup

Short owner-routed callback when the main line forwarded late

Hi [First Name] — this is [Owner Name] with [Business Name]. I saw your call come through late.

Text me your address and a quick note on what is going on, and I'll point you to the fastest next step.
  • Built for shops where the owner still catches overflow after hours.
  • Helps preserve responsiveness without sounding overly corporate.
